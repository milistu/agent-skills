# Designing for iOS

Fundamental device characteristics and patterns that distinguish the iOS (iPhone) experience.

## Device Characteristics

- **Display:** Medium-size, high-resolution display
- **Ergonomics:** Held in one or both hands, landscape and portrait orientations, viewing distance typically 1-2 feet
- **Inputs:** Multi-Touch gestures, virtual keyboards, voice control, gyroscope/accelerometer, spatial interactions, personal data (with permission)
- **App interactions:** Usage ranges from quick 1-2 minute sessions (checking updates, tracking data, messaging) to extended sessions (browsing, gaming, media). People keep multiple apps open and switch frequently.

## System Features

- Widgets
- Home Screen quick actions
- Spotlight search
- Shortcuts (Siri)
- Activity views

## Best Practices

- **Limit onscreen controls** to help people focus on primary tasks and content. Make secondary details and actions discoverable with minimal interaction.
- **Adapt seamlessly to appearance changes** — device orientation, Dark Mode, Dynamic Type — letting people choose configurations that work best for them.
- **Support natural device holding patterns.** Controls in the middle or bottom of the display are easier to reach. Let people swipe to navigate back or initiate actions in list rows.
- **Integrate platform capabilities with permission** to enhance the experience without requiring manual data entry. Examples: accept payments, use biometric authentication, leverage device location.
