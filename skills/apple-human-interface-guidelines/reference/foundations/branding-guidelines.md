# Branding Guidelines

Guidance for expressing brand identity in Apple platform apps while maintaining platform consistency.

## Best Practices

- **Use your brand's unique voice and tone** in all written communication. Convey brand personality through word choice, punctuation, and sentence structure.

- **Consider choosing an accent color.** The system applies it to interface icons, buttons, and text. In macOS, users can override it with their own accent color. See Color guidelines.

- **Consider using a custom font.** Ensure it's legible at all sizes and supports accessibility features (bold text, larger type). Use custom fonts for headlines/subheadings and system fonts for body/captions — system fonts are optimized for legibility at small sizes.

- **Branding must always defer to content.** Don't waste screen space on brand-only elements that reduce room for useful content.

- **Use standard patterns consistently.** Place UI components in expected locations and use standard symbols for common actions, even in highly stylized interfaces.

- **Don't display your logo throughout the app** unless essential for context. People know which app they're using.

- **Don't use launch screens for branding.** They disappear too quickly. Instead, consider a welcome or onboarding screen for branding content.

- **Follow Apple's trademark guidelines.** Apple trademarks must not appear in your app name or images. See [Apple Trademark List](https://www.apple.com/legal/intellectual-property/trademark/appletmlist.html) and [Guidelines for Using Apple Trademarks](https://www.apple.com/legal/intellectual-property/guidelinesfor3rdparties.html).

## Platform Considerations

No platform-specific additional considerations for iOS, iPadOS, macOS, tvOS, visionOS, or watchOS.
