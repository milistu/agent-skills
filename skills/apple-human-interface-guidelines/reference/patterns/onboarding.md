# Onboarding

Guidelines for helping users get started with your app or game. Onboarding occurs after launching is complete — it is not part of the launch experience.

## Best Practices

- **Teach through interactivity.** Let people perform tasks rather than just viewing instructions. Provide a safe environment to test actions, discover features, or try game mechanics.
- **Prefer context-specific tips over a single onboarding flow.** Integrate contextually relevant tips so users learn while making progress. Display instructions near the relevant interface area. See Apple's TipKit framework.
- **Keep prerequisite onboarding brief and enjoyable.** Don't require memorizing large amounts of information. Quick, entertaining flows have higher completion rates.
- **Make separate tutorials optional.** Let users skip it on first launch. Don't re-present it on subsequent launches, but keep it accessible (e.g., in help, account, or settings).
- **Focus on your experience.** Don't teach system or device usage — only your app/game.

## Additional Content

- **Splash screens should be brief and beautiful.** Display just long enough for users to absorb information at a glance without feeling delayed.
- **Don't let large downloads block onboarding.** Include enough media in your software package so users can start interacting immediately after launch.
- **Avoid licensing details in onboarding.** Let the App Store handle agreements/disclaimers. If you must include them, integrate in a balanced, non-disruptive way.

## Additional Requests

- **Postpone nonessential setup.** Provide reasonable defaults so users can start immediately without extra configuration.
- **Integrate essential permission requests into onboarding.** If the app needs private data to function, request during onboarding to explain why and show benefits. Otherwise, request when users first access the specific function.
- **Let users experience the app before prompting for ratings or purchases.** Engagement increases the likelihood of positive responses.

## Platform Considerations

No additional platform-specific considerations for iOS, iPadOS, macOS, tvOS, visionOS, or watchOS.
